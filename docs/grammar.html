Γραμματικὴ κατὰ κεφάλαιον

Grammar by Chapter

Every construction is introduced inductively — encountered in context before it could be stated as a rule, and recycled until automatic. This table records what each chapter formally introduces and, where applicable, what it first occasions in the narrative. Chapters not yet drafted show their planned grammar and are marked forthcoming.

Chapter Grammar introduced Notes
Phase 1 — Nominal (Chapters 1–5)
Ch 1 εἰμί (all persons, sg & pl); nominative case; article ὁ/ἡ/τό; 1st & 2nd declension nominative & accusative; adjective agreement (μέγας, μικρός, πολύς); ἐν + dative; ἐγγύς/εἰς/ἀπό + genitive; particles καί, δέ, μέν, ἀλλά, γάρ, ναί, οὐχί, ἆρα; interrogatives ποῦ and τί Genitive and dative occur passively in prepositional phrases before their formal case introduction. Chrysippus (ὁ Χρύσιππος) first appears at the end of Ch 1; no verb beyond εἰμί.
Ch 2 Accusative case (full 1st & 2nd decl.); present indicative active (3rd sg & pl); transitive verb + direct object; indefinite pronoun τις/τι (forthcoming) Forthcoming. First transitive verbs introduced; Chrysippus acts for the first time.
Ch 3 Dative case (1st & 2nd decl.); indirect object; λέγω + dative; present indicative active (1st & 2nd persons) (forthcoming) Forthcoming. First direct speech in the narrative.
Ch 4 Personal pronouns ἐγώ/σύ (nom, acc, gen, dat); demonstrative οὗτος/αὕτη/τοῦτο; ἐκεῖνος/ἐκείνη/ἐκεῖνο (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 5 3rd person pronoun αὐτός/αὐτή/αὐτό in all cases; possessive use; review of 1st & 2nd declension across all four cases (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Phase 2 — Genitive & Dative; 3rd Declension (Chapters 6–10)
Ch 6 3rd declension (consonant stems: σώματ-, ὀνοματ-); formal genitive constructions — possession, separation, partitive (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 7 3rd declension (i-stems; neuters); genitive of description; extended μέν…δέ contrast (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 8 Present indicative middle/passive; common deponents (ἔρχομαι, γίνομαι, ἀποκρίνομαι) (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 9 Imperfect indicative active (including ἦν/ἦσαν); narrative imperfect; augment patterns (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 10 Relative pronoun ὅς/ἥ/ὅ; relative clause; antecedent agreement in gender, number, case (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Phase 3 — Aorist (Chapters 11–15)
Ch 11 1st aorist indicative active (sigmatic: ἔλυσα type); aorist as completion aspect (forthcoming) Forthcoming. The "critical unlock" — narrative gains completed action for the first time.
Ch 12 2nd (strong) aorist active (εἶπον, ἦλθον, εἶδον, ἔλαβον); suppletive aorists (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 13 Aorist indicative middle; liquid aorists (ἔμεινα, ἤγγειλα); deponent aorists (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 14 Aorist indicative passive (θη-aorist: ἐλύθην); 2nd aorist passive (ἐγράφην) (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 15 Future indicative active & middle; liquid futures; εἶναι as complementary infinitive; infinitive of purpose after verbs of motion (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Phase 4 — Participles & Subjunctive (Chapters 16–20)
Ch 16 Present active participle (λύων/λύουσα/λύον); attributive and circumstantial use; temporal circumstantial (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 17 Aorist active participle; aorist passive participle; causal and concessive circumstantial (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 18 Present middle/passive participle; substantival participle (ὁ λέγων = "the one who says") (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 19 Subjunctive mood (present & aorist); ἵνα + subjunctive (purpose and content clauses); ἐάν + subjunctive (conditional) (forthcoming) Forthcoming. ἵνα replaces the infinitive for purpose in Koine — introduced here against the established infinitive construction.
Ch 20 Imperative mood (present & aorist, active & middle); prohibitions (μή + pres. imp. / μή + aor. subj.); articular infinitive (τὸ + inf.) (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Phase 5 — Perfect & Advanced (Chapters 21–25)
Ch 21 Perfect indicative active; reduplication patterns; stative aspect vs. aorist (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 22 Perfect indicative middle/passive; perfect participle (active & middle/passive) (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 23 Genitive absolute construction; dangling genitive absolute in NT usage (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 24 1st class conditional (εἰ + indicative, both real and rhetorical); 3rd class conditional (ἐάν + subjunctive) (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 25 ὅτι recitative (indirect discourse after λέγω, γινώσκω, etc.); distinction from ὅτι causal (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Phase 6 — NT-Specific Constructions (Chapters 26–28)
Ch 26 Periphrastic construction (ἦν/εἰσίν + participle); distinction from simple finite verb (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 27 Pluperfect indicative active & middle/passive; rare in NT, introduced for completeness (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 28 Hebraistic parataxis (καί-coordination as narrative sequencing; asyndeton); accusative + infinitive indirect discourse (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Phase 7 — Optative (Chapters 29–30)
Ch 29 Optative mood: wish optative (εἴθε + opt.; μὴ γένοιτο; εἴη); voluntative optative; 3rd person optative wishes (forthcoming) Forthcoming. Introduced late and explicitly anchored on the established subjunctive, which it parallels in form.
Ch 30 Potential optative (ἄν + opt.); 4th class conditional (εἰ + opt.); review of all moods and the complete verbal system (forthcoming) Forthcoming. Grammar complete after this chapter.
Phase 8 — Real NT Texts (Chapters 31–35)
Ch 31 1 John 1–2 (unadapted Greek); marginal glosses for sub-500 vocabulary only (forthcoming) Forthcoming. 1 John chosen first: simple syntax, very high NT-500 density, short paratactic sentences — the ideal bridge from narrative to real text.
Ch 32 1 John 3–5 (unadapted) (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 33 Mark: selected healing narratives (unadapted); introduction to Markan style and vocabulary (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 34 Mark: selected parables and passion narrative (unadapted) (forthcoming) Forthcoming.
Ch 35 1 Corinthians 13 (unadapted); retrospective review of NT-500 vocabulary coverage across all chapters (forthcoming) Forthcoming. Final chapter. The reader has arrived.
