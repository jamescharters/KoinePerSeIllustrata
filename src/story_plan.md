# KPSI Story Plan — Ἡ Κοινὴ καθ᾿ αὑτὴν φωτιζομένη

## Narrative premise

Corinth, 51 AD. **Χρύσιππος** (Chrysippus) is a Greek freedman in his mid-thirties
— formerly a slave in the household of a Roman merchant, now freed and running a
modest stall in the Corinthian agora selling rope, lamp oil, and odds and ends
imported from the East. He is not wealthy. He is not clever in the way he thinks he
is. He is earnest, easily distracted, occasionally pompous, and fundamentally
decent. He is the kind of man who has strong opinions about which harbour is best
and who will share those opinions at length with anyone who will listen.

His household is small. His world is the agora, the harbour at Lechaion, the road
east to Cenchreae, the tavern of one **Δάμων** (Damon), and — increasingly — the
gathering of a strange new group in the house of **Κρίσπος** (Crispus), a synagogue
official he barely knows.

The comedy is low-register: misunderstandings, bad deals, social embarrassments,
domestic disputes about a donkey, a persistent creditor named **Θεόφραστος**
(Theophrastus). The theology arrives slowly, absorbed before it is understood.

## Narrative arc (30 chapters of original text)

### Act I — Corinth (Chapters 1–10): *Who is Chrysippus?*

**Tone:** comic, grounded, domestic. Grammar: εἰμί → present active → dative/genitive → 3rd decl. → relative pronoun.

- Ch 1: The Roman world. Chrysippus introduced at the end as a man in a large city.
- Ch 2: The agora. Chrysippus's stall; his goods; his neighbours. First transitive verbs.
- Ch 3: The household. Melitta, Xanthias, Lykos. Direct speech begins.
- Ch 4: The harbour of Lechaion. Ships arrive; Chrysippus negotiates badly.
- Ch 5: A creditor arrives. Theophrastus wants money. Chrysippus has none.
- Ch 6: Chrysippus borrows from Damon. Damon's tavern; its regulars. 3rd decl. introduced.
- Ch 7: Market day. A dispute about the price of oil. Chrysippus loses.
- Ch 8: Melitta sends Chrysippus to buy bread. He buys the wrong thing. Middle/passive introduced.
- Ch 9: Xanthias runs away (briefly). The imperfect is needed to tell the story.
- Ch 10: Chrysippus hears about a new group meeting in the city. Relative pronoun; curiosity.

### Act II — The community (Chapters 11–20): *Who are these people?*

**Tone:** shifts from purely comic to comic-serious. The Pauline community enters.
Grammar: aorist (the critical unlock) → future → participles → ἵνα + subjunctive → imperatives.

- Ch 11: Chrysippus visits the harbour at Cenchreae. He meets Aquila. Aorist introduced.
- Ch 12: Aquila and Priscilla's workshop. Chrysippus is offered work. He declines.
- Ch 13: Theophrastus confronts Chrysippus publicly in the agora. Aorist middle/passive.
- Ch 14: Melitta visits Priscilla. She comes home changed. Chrysippus is suspicious.
- Ch 15: A ship from Ephesus arrives with bad news: a debt Chrysippus forgot he had. Future.
- Ch 16: Chrysippus attends the synagogue by accident. Crispus reads. Present participles.
- Ch 17: Chrysippus leaves quickly. But he remembers what was read. Aorist participles.
- Ch 18: Damon gets sick. Chrysippus visits. He doesn't know what to say. Substantival participle.
- Ch 19: Melitta tells Chrysippus to go back to the meeting. ἵνα + subjunctive.
- Ch 20: Paul arrives in Corinth. Chrysippus sees him in the agora. Imperatives; articular infinitive.

### Act III — Conviction (Chapters 21–28): *What is true?*

**Tone:** serious with comic relief. Chrysippus is being changed without fully consenting to it.
Grammar: perfect → genitive absolute → conditionals → ὅτι discourse → periphrastic → Hebraistic parataxis.

- Ch 21: Chrysippus attends a full gathering. He hears Paul speak. Perfect indicative.
- Ch 22: A crisis: Sosthenes is beaten before the governor (Acts 18:17). Chrysippus watches. Perfect M/P; perfect participle.
- Ch 23: Crispus's household is baptised (Acts 18:8). Chrysippus is outside the door. Genitive absolute.
- Ch 24: Theophrastus's daughter is ill. Chrysippus doesn't know what to do. Conditionals.
- Ch 25: Paul teaches about love (1 Cor 13 in summary). Chrysippus reports to Melitta. ὅτι discourse.
- Ch 26: Melitta decides. The household discussion. Periphrastic constructions.
- Ch 27: Damon gets better. No one can explain why. Pluperfect.
- Ch 28: Stephanas's household. Parataxis; the language of testimony.

### Act IV — Arrival (Chapters 29–30): *What now?*

- Ch 29: Chrysippus prays for the first time (optative wishes: εἴη, μὴ γένοιτο).
- Ch 30: Chloe's people arrive with letters. The grammar is now complete. Paul leaves Corinth (Acts 18:18). Chrysippus watches the ship depart.

### Act V — Real texts (Chapters 31–35)

- Ch 31–32: 1 John 1–5 (unadapted).
- Ch 33–34: Mark — selected narratives.
- Ch 35: 1 Corinthians 13 (unadapted). The reader has arrived.

## Chapter-by-chapter plan: Acts I–II in detail

### Chapter 1 — Ἡ οἰκουμένη (The inhabited world)
**Grammar:** εἰμί all forms; nom case; article; 1st/2nd decl. nom; prepositional phrases with dat/gen (passive); μέγας, μικρός, πολύς; particles.
**Scene:** Geography → Roman world → Corinth → Chrysippus (one paragraph, three sentences).
**Tone:** encyclopaedic, gradually human.
**Vocab introduced:** ὁ/ἡ/τό, εἰμί, ἡ Ἑλλάς, ἡ πόλις, ὁ ἄνθρωπος, ἡ θάλασσα, ἡ ὁδός, τὸ πλοῖον, ἡ ἐπαρχία, ἡ ἀρχή, ἐγγύς, μέγας, μικρός, πολύς, ναί, οὐχί, ποῦ, τί, ἆρα, καί, δέ, μέν, ἀλλά, γάρ.

### Chapter 2 — Ἡ ἀγορά (The market)
**Grammar:** Accusative (1st/2nd decl.); present indicative active (3rd sg/pl first, then all persons by end); basic transitive verbs (ἔχω, βλέπω, ἀγοράζω, πωλέω, φέρω); τίς/τί interrogative.
**Scene:** The Corinthian agora on a weekday morning. Chrysippus opens his stall. He has rope, lamp-oil, a jar of something he isn't sure about. His neighbour **Νίκων** (Nikon), a sardine-seller, greets him with contempt. A woman examines one of his ropes and puts it down. Chrysippus has opinions about this.
**Comedy:** Chrysippus tries to sell the unknown jar. He doesn't know what is in it. Nikon says it is fish paste. Chrysippus insists it is not. A customer buys it for very little.
**New vocab (15–17):** ἡ ἀγορά, ἔχω, βλέπω, φέρω, ὁ ἄρτος, τὸ σκεῦος, ὁ λόγος, ἡ τιμή, μέγας (acc), ὁ κύριος, ἡ γυνή, λαμβάνω, τίς/τί (interrog.), οὐδείς, πολύς (acc).
**Recycled from Ch1:** πόλις, ἄνθρωπος, εἰμί, μέγας, μικρός, ὁδός, Κόρινθος.
**Narrative hook:** Chrysippus hears that a man named Aquila has arrived from Rome and is looking for workspace in the agora.

---

### Chapter 3 — Ἡ οἰκία (The household)
**Grammar:** Dative (1st/2nd decl.); indirect object; λέγω/λαλέω + dative; 1st/2nd person present (ἔχεις, ἔχομεν, λέγω, λέγεις); vocative.
**Scene:** Chrysippus comes home. The house: one room for goods, a courtyard, a cooking fire. Melitta is cooking. Xanthias is supposedly sweeping (he is not sweeping). Lykos the donkey is in the courtyard for no clear reason.
**Comedy:** Chrysippus tells Melitta he almost sold a mystery jar for a good price. She says: you sold it for almost nothing. He says: yes, but almost. Lykos eats something he shouldn't. Xanthias is blamed.
**New vocab (15–17):** ἡ οἰκία, ὁ οἶκος, λέγω, λαλέω, ἀκούω, ἡ θύρα, ἡ αὐλή, τὸ ὕδωρ, ὁ δοῦλος, ὁ δεσπότης, ἡ κεφαλή, ὁ ὄνος, καλέω, εἰσέρχομαι (stem only, not conjugated fully yet), ἴδε/ἰδού.
**Recycled from Ch1–2:** ἄνθρωπος, γυνή, κύριος, λαμβάνω, εἰμί, λόγος.
**Narrative hook:** Melitta has heard from a neighbour that a Jewish couple — tentmakers — have taken a workshop near the agora. She is curious. Chrysippus is not curious.

---

### Chapter 4 — Τὸ λιμάνιον (The harbour)
**Grammar:** Genitive (1st/2nd decl.): possession, source, separation; ἐκ/ἀπό + gen expanded; comparative adjective with ἤ; πρός + acc (toward/to).
**Scene:** Chrysippus goes to Lechaion, Corinth's western harbour, to meet a ship from Ephesus that (he believes) carries the rope he ordered. It does not. It carries a large quantity of cloth. He is shown a document. He cannot read all of it. He negotiates.
**Comedy:** The ship's agent is Greek but speaks with an Egyptian accent Chrysippus finds impenetrable. Xanthias translates, badly. The donkey Lykos, brought to carry rope, now has to carry cloth instead. He expresses displeasure.
**New vocab (15–17):** τὸ πλοῖον (acc/gen now active), ὁ λιμήν, ἐκ, ὁ ἔμπορος, τὸ ἱμάτιον, ὁ δοῦλος (gen/dat now), ἡ ἐπιστολή, γράφω, ἀναγινώσκω, ὁ ἄγγελος (= messenger here, not yet "angel"), μικρός (comp: μικρότερος), πλείων, νῦν, τότε, ἐκεῖ.
**Recycled:** πλοῖον, θάλασσα, ὁδός, ἔχω, φέρω, γυνή, οἰκία.
**Narrative hook:** At the harbour Chrysippus sees Aquila — the man from Rome — examining a cargo. They do not speak, but Chrysippus notices he is a careful, unhurried man.

---

### Chapter 5 — Ὁ χρεώστης (The debtor)
**Grammar:** Personal pronouns full (ἐγώ, σύ, ἡμεῖς, ὑμεῖς) in all cases; demonstratives οὗτος/αὕτη/τοῦτο; ἐκεῖνος; αὐτός as emphatic "self."
**Scene:** Theophrastus arrives at Chrysippus's stall. He is a respectable man with a wax tablet on which things are written. He is owed money. Chrysippus owes him money. This is not a surprise to either of them but it is still unpleasant.
**Comedy:** Theophrastus enumerates the debt formally. Chrysippus has counter-arguments, none of them good. Melitta appears and agrees with Theophrastus about everything. Chrysippus feels betrayed. Xanthias, asked his opinion, offers none, but his face says enough. Theophrastus leaves. Chrysippus reassures himself that he has a plan.
**New vocab (15–17):** ἐγώ/σύ (all cases), ἡμεῖς/ὑμεῖς, οὗτος/αὕτη/τοῦτο, ἐκεῖνος, αὐτός (emphatic), ὁ χρεώστης [gloss: debtor — not NT-500], τὸ ἀργύριον, ὀφείλω, σήμερον, αὔριον, οὐκέτι, ὅλος, ἀλήθεια.
**Recycled:** λέγω, ἀκούω, γυνή, δοῦλος, κύριος, ἔχω, θύρα, οἰκία.
**Narrative hook:** Theophrastus, leaving, pauses and says that he heard the Jewish tentmaker gave a short address at the synagogue. He says this not warmly, but not coldly either. It is information.

## Vocabulary policy

- **Primary constraint:** NT Top-500 lemmas by frequency.
- **Extended vocab (LXX/Didache/Apostolic Fathers):** Permitted *only* when NT vocab alone cannot carry the narrative. Must be:
  1. Glossed with `.gloss` tooltip in HTML.
  2. Noted in the αἱ λέξεις section with provenance (e.g. "[LXX]").
  3. Never introduced at the expense of NT-500 recycling targets.
- **Recycling rule:** Every introduced word appears 5–8× in its introduction chapter, then every 2–3 chapters thereafter. Atrophy threshold: 4+ chapters without appearance = flagged.

## Quality standard

- Morphologically correct Koine throughout (BDF + Wallace).
- Fully polytonic, correctly accented.
- Authentic Koine syntax: ἵνα not infinitive for purpose; ὅτι recitative; Hebraistic parataxis where narratively natural.
- No Atticism: no classical optative in place of subjunctive for conditions; no classical negation patterns.
